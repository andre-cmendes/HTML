About




Foxes are small to medium-sized omnivorous mammals belonging to several genera of the family Canidae.
Foxes have flat skulls, upright triangular ears, a pointed, slightly upturned snout, and a long, bushy tail.
Twelve species belong to the monophyletic group of "true foxes" in the genus Vulpes.
Approximately another 25 current or extinct species are part of the paraphyletic group of South American foxes or the peripheral group, which consists of the bat-eared fox, gray fox and island fox.
Foxes live on every continent except Antarctica. By far the most common and widespread species of fox is the red fox (Vulpes vulpes) with around 47 recognized subspecies.
The global distribution of foxes, along with their widespread reliance on cunning, has developed to their prominence in popular culture and is not folklore in many societies around the world.
Fox hunting with packs of hounds, long a demanded activity in Europe, especially the British Isles, was exported by European settlers to various parts of the New World.
